EXTREME WEATHER VISUALIZATION FOR CHANGE:
ACTION OR INACTION

Profs. Ronald Baecker and John Danahy
Dynamic Graphics Project, University of Toronto
13 November 2025

The world is confronted with possibly catastrophic harm from greenhouse gas emissions. Global surface temperatures continue their unprecedented rise. There are more frequent and severe extreme weather events such as floods and fires.

Yet society has not acted sufficiently. We aim to visualize real extreme weather events to show how there could be more destruction, and how actions could reduce the damage.

Case Study: Mississauga's Little Etobicoke Creek

We have simulated and visualized a 100-year flood scenario for Little Etobicoke Creek (Mississauga) under different mitigation scenarios. The figure below illustrates how flood damage impacts could be reduced through the mitigation measures tested in this study, including channel deepening and widening, as well as enhanced upstream infiltration.
